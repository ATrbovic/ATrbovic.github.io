Current Research

Quantum Game Theory & Strategic Optimization

My doctoral research investigates how quantum entanglement and superposition can provide strategic advantages in competitive scenarios. I'm particularly interested in nonlocal games such as the CHSH game, where quantum strategies can outperform any classical approach.

Using Qiskit and custom quantum simulators, I explore how these quantum mechanical properties might inform strategic thinking in broader contexts, from supply chain optimization to collaborative decision-making in distributed systems.

Featured Work

Dynamic Tackler Identification System

Developed an AI system using convolutional neural networks to predict the most likely tackler in NFL plays. The system processes real-time player positioning data and achieves significant improvements in tactical analysis accuracy. This work demonstrates the application of deep learning to complex spatial-temporal prediction problems.

NFL Replay Visualizer

Built an interactive C++ application using SFML for analyzing NFL game data. The system integrates with PostgreSQL databases to provide real-time visualization of player movements and strategic formations, reducing analysis runtime by 63%.
